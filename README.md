## Repeatable workflows culturomics

Copyright (C) 2026 Theresa Henke, Ana Novoa
https://onestop-project.eu/

Two repeatable culturomics workflows were developed as part of the Horizon Europe project OneSTOP (One Biosecurity Systems and Technology for People, Places, and Pathways) to address the human dimensions of biological invasions. 
Specifically, developed workflows to i) assess public interest in Inavasive Alien Species (IAS) as reflected in the dynamics of online search activity, and ii) analyze the presence and dynamics of IAS in e-commerce as a proxy for potential introduction pathways.

The workflows have been developed and will be showcased on the basis of two case studies. In case study 1, we analysed public interest in the 88 species listed on the list of Union concern between 2016 and 2022 
in all 27 current EU member states (MS) as well as the UK. We compiled weekly search-query values for each species-country combination. We conducted a peak analysis of the data and identified a total of 153 peaks that were included for further assessment.
For each of these peaks we performed a manual search on Google search enginge in the adjacent time period for the context(s) in which the species was mentioned. Using pre defined categories, we documented information on the specific angles and topics of the content covered, the platforms it was shared 
on, and the stakeholders associated. In case study 2, we assessed the presence of IAS listed on or proposed for the Union list of concern in e-commerce. Online retailers and Peer-to-peer trading platforms, two common e-commerce platforms, were surveyed in 6 EU member
states (Czech Republic, France, Germany, Italy, the Netherlands, Spain) as well as internationally in 2021 and 2024. 

Both workflows and associated R codes are available in this repository.


This repository is still a work in progress.
